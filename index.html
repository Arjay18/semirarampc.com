Semirara Multi-Purpose Cooperative

Our Story

The SEMIRARA MULTI-PURPOSE COOPERATIVE, commonly known as SEMCO, commenced its operation with its principal office established at Semirara Island, Caluya, Antique. Its roots date back to the early 80s when Semirara Coal Corporation (SCC) was in its initial stage of mining. Originally established as the Semirara Employees Fund (SEF), SEMCO provided essential savings and lending services for SCC's regular employees.

On March 15, 1990, the cooperative transformed into Semirara Coal Corporation Employees Multi-Purpose Cooperative Inc. (SEMCO INC) upon registration with the Bureau of Agricultural Cooperative Development (BACOD) under Certificate of Registration No. R-VI-FF-468-RR. At that time, SEMCO reported Members Equity of P746,775, Total Assets of P2,141,202, a Net Surplus of P107,057, and a Cooperative Membership of 730.


In 1991, the amended name was confirmed via the CDA Certificate of Confirmation No. ILO C-1292 under Republic Act No. 6938 as a Primary Cooperative, Multi-Purpose/Non-Agricultural Cooperative. Despite challenges brought about by changes in SCC’s management in 1998—which affected membership and equity—SEMCO adapted to the evolving economic landscape.


Entering the new millennium, renewed support by the management of Semirara Mining and Power Corporation (SMPC) via payroll deductions helped SEMCO regain momentum. Following several amendments to its By-Laws, on December 4, 2006, the cooperative proudly adopted its current name—SEMIRARA MULTI-PURPOSE COOPERATIVE (SEMCO)—and was duly re-registered under the Philippine Cooperative Code 2008 (Republic Act 9520) on December 1, 2009.


Today, SEMCO’s membership has expanded beyond SMPC employees to include island-based private and public school teachers, school staff, business owners, market vendors, and Operator-members of SEMTODA.
